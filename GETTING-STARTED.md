# Getting started with Brainerd + AI

## Step 1: Open your AI assistant

Open Claude Code (or any AI coding assistant) pointed at your WordPress
install where Brainerd theme, Brainerd Blocks, and Brainerd Companion
are installed.

## Step 2: Paste this prompt

Copy and paste this as your first message:

---

I want to build a new website using the Brainerd theme. Before exploring
any code or proposing a plan, read the CLAUDE.md file in the theme
directory (wp-content/themes/brainerd/CLAUDE.md) and follow the mandatory
discovery conversation process described there.

Ask me about my business, my brand, and my goals before making any
technical decisions. Do not pick colors, fonts, or page layouts until
we've talked through the discovery questions. I may not have everything
figured out yet — that's fine, use defaults for anything I'm unsure about.

Here's what I know so far about the project:

**Business name:** [your business name]
**What we do:** [brief description]
**Location:** [city, state or "online only"]
**Pages I think I need:** [home, about, services, contact, etc.]
**Existing brand assets:** [logo? colors? fonts? or "starting from scratch"]
**Anything else:** [any other context — sites you like, features you need, etc.]

---

## Step 3: Have a conversation

The AI will ask you follow-up questions about your brand, audience, and
goals. Answer what you can, say "I don't know yet" for anything you're
unsure about. The theme works with placeholder values while you figure
things out.

## Step 4: Build

Once discovery is complete, the AI will propose a plan and start building
your site using the Brainerd block library and design system. Everything
is file-based and reproducible — no vendor lock-in, no proprietary tools.

## What you'll need eventually (but not right now)

- A logo (SVG or high-res PNG with transparent background)
- Brand colors (hex codes or general direction)
- Content for your pages (text, photos, bios)
- A domain name and hosting

None of these are required to start building. The theme ships with
sensible defaults for everything.
